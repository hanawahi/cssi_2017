Alcohol and Drug Abuse

Alcohol

Alcohol is the leading cause of death for college students than all other drugs combined.

Most college students have a lot of pressure to drink, especially when trying to make friends. According to the National Institues of Health (NIH), about 50% college sudents have reported to binge drink, which is defined as drinking too much in a short period of time.

The video below shows the "science" of being addicted to alcohol:

Alcohol can not only cause problems in your health, but it can also cause trouble in your academics, relationships, and families.
The following are consequences you can face if you drink too much:

Social-Related:
-sexual assult
-date rape
-commiting criminal activities
Academic-Related:
-performing poorly in class
-lower concentration levels
-little to no participation in class or extra cirricular activities
Health-Related:
-Liver Damage
-High Blood Pressure
-Alcohol Use Disorder (AUD)
-Death

What Can I Do to Stop This Addiction?

  • Know your alcoholic craving triggers
  • Eat a balanced diet
  • Exercise
  • Keep a journal and log how many times you drink per day
  • Set a goal for yourself, but do not quit all at once. Plan days when you will allow yourself to drink and days when you will not drink at all
  • Beware of the consequences

    Recognizing Addiction:

    Here are a few warning signs to look for if you think that you're addicted to drugs:

    • Poor academic performance
    • Drastic changes in weight
    • Isolation
    • Trouble with the law
    • Agitation
    • Skipping classes
    • Lack of motivation
    • Forgetfulness

    What if this costs me too much money?

    Your health insurance may cover substance abuse treatment services. The Mental Health Parity and Addiction Equity Act under the Affordable Care Act ensures that health plan features like co-pays, deductibles, and visit limits are generally not more restrictive for mental health and substance abuse disorder benefits than they are for medical and surgical benefits.
