Anxiety

College students throughout the world face anxiety in one shape of form or another. It can be through worrying for an upcoming test to starting a new job.

>What are the signs and symptoms of anxiety?

  • Generalized Anxiety Disorder: People display excessive anxiety or worry for months and face several anxiety-related symptoms. Symptoms include muscle tension, being easily fatigued, difficulty concentration, sleep problems.
  • Panic Disorder: Recurrent unexpected panic attacks, which are sudden periods of intense fear that may include palpitations, pounding heart, or accelerated heart rate; sweating
  • Social Anxiety Disorder: a marked fear of social or performance situations in which they expect to feel embarrassed, judged, rejected, or fearful of offending others.

>What treatments will I be expecting to get?

Anxiety disorders are treated with pyschotherapy, medication, or both.

>Pyschotherapy:

  • Cognitive Behavioral Therapy: Teaches a person different ways of thinking, behaving, and reacting to anxiety-producing and fearful situations
  • Self Help Groups: Sharing problmes and expressing yourself is a great way to release stess and get some postive feedback.
  • Stress-Management Techniques: Avoiding caffine, exercising, and breathing exercises are well known to help cure anxiety.
