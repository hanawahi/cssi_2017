Eating Disorders

Transitional periods have often been identified as triggers in those who may be struggling with an eating disorder, and students are not exempt from the vulnerabilities that may be experienced as they start the school year, especially if they are barley entering college.

Types of Eating Disorders:

  • Anorexia- Students often avoid eating, so that they can become extremely thin. Sights include: an intense drive for thinness, fear of becoming fat, and distorted body image
  • Bulimia- Students typically â€œbinge and purge," meaning eating a lot of food, and then vomitting it out
  • Binge eating- Uncontrollable, excessive eating, followed by feelings of shame and guilt

Types of Eating Disorder Treatment:

  • Outpatient: Includes see a nutritionist, therapist and other recovery professional
  • Intensive Outpatient: Includes individualized therapy, personalized nutrition consultation, topic-focused groups, and/or family support groups
  • Inpatient / Hospital Treatment: 24/7 care monitoring your food intake
  • Entertainment: Art Therapy, Dance Movement Therapy
