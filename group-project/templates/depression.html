Depression

Today, many college students are going through depression. Many students, including first years, are having trouble adjusting to their new lives or miss their home and families. You are not alone!
According to American Psychological Association, more than 1/3 of students in the US have gone through depression in the last year. The following infografic puts the statistic in perspective:

It is important to recognize that depression is a mental health issue, and it is important that you get help.

Different Types of Depression:

Depression comes in all different kinds of forms:

  • Persistent sadness
  • Feelings of hopelessness
  • Feelings of guilt or worthlessness
  • Decreased energy, fatigue
  • Difficulty sleeping
  • Loss of concentration
  • Thoughts of suicide
  • Restlessness/Irritablity
  • Persistent physical symptoms, such as headaches

    • Where can I get help?

    Most colleges provide a counseling centers, student health services, or both. If you have depression that is in the way of fulflilling your school work, you should make an appointment as soon as possible. This This can be a doctor or health care provider in your school, off-campus, or in your hometown. If you're doctor finds other problems, he can refer you to a psychiatrist, counselor, or psychologist.
    It is IMPORTANT that if you have thoughts of suicide you should call 1-800-273-8255, for FREE 24-hour help, call campus security, or call 911.

    What treatment(s) will I get?

    A therapist will help you manage your feelings and moods through various methods:
    • Cognitive Behavioral Talk Therapy: Focuses on thoughts, behaviors, and feelings related to depression
    • Interpersonal Talk Therapy: focuses on working on relationships
    • Dialectical Behavior Therapy: useful when depression is accompanied by self-destructive or self-harming behavior
    • Prescribing medications: Lexapro, Cymbalta, Nardil,etc
